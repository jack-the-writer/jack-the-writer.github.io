Silence deafening as Western teams avoid Xinjiang cotton row in Tokyo

Photo by Reuters.

Published on 1 August 2021 in the South China Morning Post

Despite allegations of forced labour in Xinjiang’s cotton industry, many teams at the Tokyo Olympics still wear sportswear made by Chinese companies that have said publicly that they source their cotton from the region. Advertising that a product uses Xinjiang cotton appeals to the patriotism of Chinese consumers, but that’s considered unethical by many outside China.

My colleague Nadia Lam and I went through the catalogue of Peak Sport Products, whose CEO went on Sina Weibo to say the brand has always used Xinjiang cotton. The company sponsored several European teams and a few others in Asia and Africa.

Using product descriptions from Peak Sport and matching its catalogue with the sportswear worn by athletes, we found that the German men’s basketball team, for example, wore socks that were made in China. More than 90% of China’s cotton is produced in Xinjiang.
