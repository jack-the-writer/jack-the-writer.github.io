Exiled for 21 years in Korea: Life of Chinese dissident Wu Zhenrong

Photo by Shim Hyun-chul.

Published on 23 February 2023 in The Korea Times

This long-form article profiles Wu Zhenrong, a Chinese man who was forced to leave China in 2002 for South Korea without his family. He had tried to publish a book about the Cultural Revolution. National security police intercepted a letter he wrote to the publisher and went looking for him.

The story describes Wu’s life, from being a boy growing up poor in the early days of communist China, to becoming a Red Guard, then a soldier and finally a low-profile democracy activist. He has spent more than 20 years in South Korea living on benefits and in a small flat without a toilet. Inside, he wrote essays about democracy in China that won’t be read by most of the Chinese public. Now at 73 years old, Wu pondered about death and his legacy.

I met Wu twice for an interview. I shadowed him with the photojournalist Shim Hyun-chul as he went about his day, visiting his home, neighbourhood and favourite Chinese restaurant in Seoul.

The details of his everyday life brought out his character and were essential to illustrating the thoughts and feelings of an ageing activist exiled from home.
