How Beijing is using ‘fishing militia’ to assert its claims in the South China Sea

Illustration by Lau Ka-kuen.

Published on 23 September 2023 in the South China Morning Post

This story uses open-source intelligence heavily. It relies on ship-tracking records, satellite imagery, company records, tender notices and publicly available literature to look into the nature and behaviour of China’s maritime militia. The militia has been accused of performing dangerous manoeuvres to intimidate coastguards and fishers from the Philippines, Vietnam and other rival claimants to disputed reefs and waters in the South China Sea.

The story dovetails three missions by the Philippines to resupply its troops on the Second Thomas Shoal, which is also claimed by Beijing. On those missions, Chinese fishing boats blocked and closely surrounded the Philippine ships in tandem with the Chinese coastguard. These are confirmed by photos and video footage taken by the Phillippines’ coastguards and independently verified using ship tracks.

Using those tracks, I also traced the ports from which those fishing boats left and examined the ownership of those ports using land sale records from local Chinese authorities.

The piece also includes interviews from researchers who look closely into how China is increasingly relying on civilian forces to assert its territorial claims, blurring the lines between civilians and military and law enforcement officials.
