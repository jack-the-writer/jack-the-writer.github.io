Podcast: Xi’s Arab-China summit; Biden’s TSMC pivot in Arizona and EU EV diplomacy

Released on 10 December 2022 on the South China Morning Post website

This is one of the podcast episodes I did with the South China Morning Post’s China Geopolitics podcast to explain a news event I have been following and writing about.

Here, I talked about Chinese President Xi Jinping’s trip to Saudi Arabia to attend the first China-Arab summit.

I explained the crucial timing of the trip: The OPEC+ alliance chose to cut oil production to support prices, bringing it at odds with the Joe Biden administration. The US government said the decision would harm the global economy and support Russia’s economy when the country was at war with Ukraine. It also happened when Saudi Arabia was trying to join the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation, a Beijing-led Eurasian economic and security grouping. Gulf states were also trying to diversify their oil-dependent economies with the help of Chinese investments.

This episode was produced after I had written about Xi’s trip to Riyadh in a story that drew on analyses by experts inside and outside China.
