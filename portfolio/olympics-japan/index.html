Tokyo Olympics: Anti-Japanese sentiment spills over as angry Chinese netizens rail against athletes who beat their compatriots

Photo by Reuters.

Published on 31 July 2021 in the South China Morning Post

I wrote this story when I was seconded to the South China Morning Post’s Sports Desk to cover the 2020 Tokyo Olympics held in 2021.

The main story was that Japan had beaten China in the mixed-doubles table tennis finals, ending China’s gold medal sweep since the 2008 Beijing Olympics. Chinese spectators reacted strongly against the Japanese victory, accusing without evidence the Japanese team of breaking rules or even verbally attacking the Japanese duo.

In the article, I quoted those comments made by Chinese social media users after verifying that they were real people by reviewing posts and photos on their profile for coherence.

The crux of the piece, however, is to tell “the story behind the story” of why Chinese spectators reacted in the way they did. In simple language, I described China’s historical resentment against Japan that still shapes their relationship today, and the rise of online nationalism and “Little Pinks” (小粉红 xiaofenhong).
